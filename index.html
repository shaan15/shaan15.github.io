Hi, I'm Shaan Chopra!

Design Research | Partipatory Design | Inclusive Design | Coding

I'm a final-year graduate student at the University of Maryland, College Park (UMD). I have experience with pretty much every step of the user research, design, and development process. I love solving user experiences problems, particulary by taking user-centered and participatory approaches. I am passionate about creating culturally-responsive, inclusive, and accessible technologies for different populations (e.g., women, children, older adults, people with chronic health conditions, people with cognitive disabilities, low literacy & economically weak groups) and envision creating a more compassionate world that supports their diverse identity facets.

I hold a bachelor's degree in Computer Science & Engineering from IIIT-Delhi during which I explored different sides of computer science (e.g., social computing, machine learning, ICTs for development) and also spent a wonderful summer interning at INRIA, Paris. I was intrigued by the sociocultural dynamics of technology and wondered what made certain applications so widely usable. Pursuing a master's degree in HCI to learn more about user experience research and technology design felt like the natural next step.

At UMD, I gaining practical skills in UX research, usability testing, and data visualization through coursework, research, and internships. I have published my research on health, personal informatics, and accessibility-related topics at top HCI venues. I spent the summer as a User Experience intern at Appruve where I collaborated with their product and engineering teams to design the future of financial onboarding and verification products. I also remotely collaborated with the Loki Team at INRIA, Lille, designing interactive widgets for touch-based interfaces that afford micro-interactions. Last but not the least, I worked as a research assistant at UMD, conducting cross-cultural studies using in-depth interviews, online diary studies, and remote usability testing.
